Egyptian Flag


The national flag of Egypt is a tricolour consisting of the three equal horizontal red, white, and black bands of the Egyptian revolutionary flag that dates back to the 1952 Egyptian Revolution. The red stands for the Sacrafices and blood of Egyptian Martyrs, the white symbolizes for Peace, and the black symbolizes for the dark period of occupation. The flag bears Egypt's national emblem, the Egyptian eagle of Saladin, centered in the white band. This Eagle represents the strength and power.
